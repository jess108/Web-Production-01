DEPTH OF FIELD

Photoshop Depth of Field

This piano seemed to me like the perfect object to create a compelling image of shallow depth of field. My class mate Aminata did a beautiful job framing the shot to capture a long row of piano keys, but the original shot had all of the keys relatively in focus, especially in the center of the row. I used the Field Blur tool to place about 30% blur in the center of the row and about a 50% blur towards the end to create the effect of shallow depth of field in Photoshop because I wanted to emphasize the length of the piano by blurring the keys in the distance.

In Camera Depth of Field

For the in-camera depth of field excercise, I was excited to come across these lockers in the hallway. Looks to me like the perfect subject to stage a shot such as this. The aperature is at f/4 with the shutter speed at 1/250s. The ISO was 1600. I didn't do too much editing to this photo. I brightened the exposure +0.30, touched up the highlights and the blacks about +10 to bring up the contrast, then I increased the vibrance and saturation around +40 in order to warm up the wood of the lockers.

RESIZING IMAGES

The 1920 X 1080 px Image

Using Photoshop, I simply changed the size of the image file to 1920 px, which automatically adjusted the height to about 1080 and the resolution to 300 ppi. When I uploaded it to my web page, however, the image was entirely too big to look at so I adjusted the width and height to 960 X 540 in order for the image to fit properly onto the screen.

The 5 X 7 Image

For the second image in the assignment, I took the original image before I resized it for the first web page of the 1920 X 1080 image and I not only cropped the image in order to make Aquafina adhere to the Rule of Thirds, I also resized the image from the menu in order to get the inches down to 5 X 7, making sure that the ppi was set to 72 ppi as the assignment instructed us to do.

The Thumbnail Image

Last but not least, for the final image in the assignment, I cropped the original image again to make it square, then I went back into the menu to resize the image to the size of a Thumbnail at 160 X 160 pixels at 72 ppi. I gave Aquafina a tiny bit of lead room in the direction that he was gazing instead of placing him directly in the center of the frame.
